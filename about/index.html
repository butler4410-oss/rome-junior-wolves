About the Pack

Rome's youth football and cheerleading program — building athletes, character, and the future of Rome Wolves football.

Our Mission

Stronger Kids. Stronger Families. Stronger Community.

To build stronger kids, stronger families, and a stronger community through athletics by teaching lifelong lessons of discipline, health, teamwork, and resilience. We use sports as a platform to help young people thrive physically, mentally, and emotionally — on the field and beyond.

Fear the Pack isn't just a slogan — it's the standard we hold each other to.

Our Vision

To be the leading youth athletic organization in Northwest Georgia, recognized for developing champions in life as well as in sport — expanding opportunities for all children to grow, compete, and succeed.

What We Stand For

Our Core Values

Youth First

Every decision prioritizes the growth, safety, and well-being of the kids we serve.

Character Through Competition

Athletics are a classroom for life lessons — discipline, resilience, respect, and accountability.

Health for Life

We promote physical fitness, proper nutrition, and mental health as the foundation for long-term success.

Inclusivity & Access

Every child deserves an opportunity to participate and grow — regardless of background, ability, or resources.

Community Impact

We exist to strengthen our town — creating a pipeline of leaders who give back to Rome and beyond.

Excellence in All Things

We teach kids to set goals, strive for excellence, and carry those standards into school, sports, and life.

Education Through Athletics

Sports aren't just about games — they're a bridge to college, careers, and higher opportunities.

The Bigger Picture

Feeding the Rome Wolves

The Junior Wolves are the official youth feeder program for the Rome High School and Rome Middle School Wolves. Same mascot. Same red and gold. Same Friday-night dream.

Every kid who starts here is taking a real step toward the field they've watched their heroes play on. We teach the systems, the culture, and the standard early — so when they reach middle and high school, they're ready.
